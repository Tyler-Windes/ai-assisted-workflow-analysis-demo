#### Business Context

##### Scenario

A fictional operations team receives workflow records from multiple intake channels: email, forms, chat messages, and API-generated alerts. The team needs to triage work, assign ownership, track status, report KPIs, and identify process improvement opportunities.

The raw intake records are useful, but they are inconsistent. Some records have missing ownership, non-standard priority values, vague descriptions, duplicate indicators, incomplete closure notes, and inconsistent system-area naming.

##### Stakeholders

- Operations manager responsible for work visibility and prioritization
- Technical analyst responsible for structuring records and validating data quality
- Reporting stakeholder responsible for KPI summaries
- Team lead responsible for triage and ownership assignment
- Process owner responsible for future-state workflow improvements

##### Current Problems

- Priority values are inconsistent across intake channels.
- Ownership is sometimes missing or unclear.
- Some closed records lack resolution notes.
- Duplicate or related records are not consistently linked.
- System-area naming is inconsistent.
- Reporting can be misleading if quality issues are not flagged first.
- AI-assisted classification could help, but only if outputs remain draft and reviewable.

##### Desired Future State

- Standardized required intake fields
- Consistent priority and status values
- Required ownership before work begins
- Required closure documentation before a record is considered complete
- Data-quality checks before KPI reporting
- Traceability from raw record to cleaned output
- Human-reviewed AI assistance for classification, extraction, and validation review
