#### Current-State Analysis

The fictional intake process shows recurring workflow and data-quality issues that would weaken reporting and prioritization if left unresolved.

##### Observed Issues

| Issue | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Inconsistent priority labels | SLA and escalation reporting become unreliable. |
| Missing assigned teams | Work can appear active without clear ownership. |
| Incomplete resolution notes | Closed items lose traceability and review value. |
| Duplicate indicators | Workload counts may be inflated. |
| Inconsistent system-area naming | Trends are harder to identify across related records. |
| Vague descriptions | Analyst review time increases because records need clarification. |

##### Current-State Risk

The main risk is not that the team lacks data. The risk is that the data appears reportable before it has been normalized and validated.

This is why the project separates raw intake, cleaned output, validation findings, SQL-style checks, and stakeholder summaries.
